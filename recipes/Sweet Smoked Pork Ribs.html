Sweet Smoked Pork Ribs

Description

Personally, there is nothing better on a hot summers day than a rack of ribs! Between St. Louis style, baby back, Dino Ribs and more, Ribs are a family favorite If you follow this simple recipe, you will have your entire neighborhood crowding around to get their hands on these. Enjoy!

Ingredients

  • Mustard
  • Your favorite Rib seasoning
  • 2 Tablespoons Salt
  • 2 Tablespoons Pepper
  • 2 Slabs Bone-In Spare Ribs
  • 8 Tablespoons Butter
  • 4 Tablespoons Honey
  • 1/2 Cup Brown Sugar
  • Your favorite Barbecue Sauce

Steps

  1. Start your smoker and preheat it to 250 degrees fahrenheit. If using charcoal, get your coals lit in a firestarter, and place the coals on one side of your grill/smoker. This creates a hot and cold zone
  2. Take your ribs and trim excess fat off of the ribs
  3. Cut a slit in the membrane on the back of the ribs. Then take a spoon and get under the membrane and detach the membrane from the ribs
  4. Take the mustard and spread it evenly over the ribs, covering the entire surface area
  5. Using your favorite rib-rub (store-bought or homemade), lay a heavy layer of the rub over the ribs. At this time you will also evenly distribute your salt and Pepper Use your hands to really work the seasoning into the ribs
  6. Cover the ribs in plastic wrap, and set in the fridge for at least two hours. Doing this will bring the moisture to the surface and dry the skin of the ribs.
  7. Place your ribs on your smoker, and if using charcoal place them in the cold side
  8. Using the 3-2-1 method, you will smoke them uncovered for 3 hours. After the 3 hour mark You will wrap them. Cut your stick of butter up into tablespoon portions and lay them out on a slab of aluminum foil. Pour some honey over the butter, along with your brown sugar. Place the ribs meat side down, wrap tightly and smoke for 2 more hours.
  9. After 2 hours, uncover your ribs. Take your Barbecue sauce and spread it over the top and bottom of the rack of ribs. Once covered in sauce, place them back on the smoker for 1 last hour to finish up
  10. Take the ribs off the smoker and let rest for at least 1 hour
  11. Apply a little more sauce, cut the ribs between the bones, and enjoy!
